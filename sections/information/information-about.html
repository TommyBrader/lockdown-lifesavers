About Lockdown Lifesavers

Lockdown Lifesavers was created to be an outlet for people stuck in lockdown as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. Lockdown has proved to be a tough time for a lot of people and Lockdown Lifesavers hopes to serve as an information source, charity fundraiser and also a place where people can find something to occupy their time without feeling like days are boring or wasted.

  • The Information section is home to information about us and the website as well as updates and charity links relating to the pandemic.
  • The Games section is home to some fun and engaging, in-browser games which don't require too much time or brain power.
  • The Activities section's purpose is to offer suggestions of what to do away from the website, whether that be film and TV reccomendations or some useful recipes and exercises to try.
  • Finally, the Shop section is home to the Lockdown Lifesavers shop. This is where you can buy Lockdown Lifesavers merchandise and also some useful equipment to help you get through lockdown. 80% of all profits go towards lockdown relief charities.

About Us

Lockdown Lifesavers was created by one person, Tommy Brader, as part of a Final Project for a Digital Media Culture MA at Birkbeck, University of London. I decided to create a website like this as it is highly relevant to the world in which I have been completing my master's. The website is also a concept broad enough to serve as a way for me to display various skills and techniques for design and coding I have learnt during my studies and also as part of research for the project.

Lockdown Lifesavers is my second attempt at designing a full website using just HTML, CSS and JavaScript. The first of which, The Penguin Information Project, can be found by clicking the link underneath the random penguin.
